What Is Money?

New money in the modern U.S. economy is created primarily through bank lending and federal borrowing. The Federal Reserve's structure, the elimination of meaningful reserve requirements, and perpetual deficit spending form a self-reinforcing loop: large banks and asset managers profit from both the creation of credit and the management of resulting government debt, while the costs — rising prices, stagnant real wages, and systemic risk — are borne by the public.

This module traces the mechanics — fractional reserve lending, the petrodollar, the broken federal budget process — and the historical turning points — gold standard abandonment, the 16th Amendment, too big to fail — that produced today's architecture.
