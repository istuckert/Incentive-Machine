Specialty Occupation Program: what the law says vs. what actually happens. The Labor Condition Application is self-attested, minimally audited, and approved 99% of the time. The result is a captive labor pool paid below market and unable to safely assert rights.

The H-1B program was designed for "specialty occupations" requiring at least a bachelor's degree in a specific field. The mechanism that makes it exploitable is structural, not illegal: the Labor Condition Application is self-attested and approved ~99% of the time. The employer sets the wage. The employer files the paperwork. The employer controls the visa.

The outsourcing model adds a second layer. TCS, Cognizant, Infosys, Wipro, and HCL sponsor H-1B workers and deploy them to third-party end-clients — banks, telecom, retail — at 30–40% below the cost of a direct hire. The worker's visa is tied to the outsourcer, not the company where they actually work. The Big Three own both the outsourcer and every major end-client.

The visa tether — one employer controls immigration status, visa renewal, and green card sponsorship simultaneously — is the structural mechanism. Workers who face deportation in 60 days cannot safely report wage theft, refuse bench time, or participate in collective action. This is not incidental to the program: it is the program.

Agricultural & seasonal workers: a different program, the same extraction. H-2A and H-2B are separate programs with distinct sponsor chains. Both run at ~99% DOL approval. Both transfer billions annually from workers and communities to operators and their institutional owners.

The triple tether is the structural mechanism of the H-2A program: a single entity simultaneously controls the worker's visa status, housing, and employment. Lose one — lose all three at the same moment. This is not a corner case. It is the stated design of the program.

Farm Labor Contractors (FLCs) — the certified sponsors — file ~15% of H-2A applications but drive the majority of debarments. DOL's approval rate is ~99%, largely self-attested with minimal audit. H-2A violations account for 54% of all agricultural back-wage assessments FY2018–2023, but annual back-wage recovery is only ~$8.3M — negligible against a 400,000+ position program.

Operation Blooming Onion (Georgia, 2015–2026): FLC operators charged with forced labor, violence, sexual assault, and worker deaths. $200M+ contractor profit. Federal prosecutors called it "modern-day slavery." The program's self-attestation mechanism made it possible.

Unlike H-2A, H-2B workers are not covered by mandatory employer housing — a different coercion structure, but the same 60-day deportation clock. In all top-15 H-2B occupations, certified wages fall below the OEWS national average. The statutory cap is 66,000 — set in 1992 and never raised by legislation. Congress has instead authorized DHS supplements every year since FY2016. FY2024 total reached 130,716.

The meatpacking expansion is the most documented employer strategy: U.S. meatpacking unionization fell from 37.4% in 1983 to 13.5% in 2024; foreign-born share rose to 42%. Employers are now lobbying Congress to expand H-2B to year-round meatpacking, currently prohibited under the seasonal program definition.
