Section 03a — Left Branch

The H-1B Visa

Skilled Worker Program: what the law says vs. what actually happens. The Labor Condition Application is self-attested, minimally audited, and approved 99% of the time. The result is a captive labor pool paid below market and unable to safely assert rights.

~730k H-1B Workers Active in U.S. Early 2026 · USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub
456,725 FY2025 Petitions Filed 328,185 approved · USCIS FY2025 Report
85k Annual Cap (Regular + Adv. Degree) 65,000 regular + 20,000 advanced degree · Cap-exempt employers: uncapped
~99% DOL LCA Approval Rate Self-attested · minimal audit · DOL Fact Sheet #26A
~72% India Share of Approvals FY2023–2025 · China ~11–12% · all others under 2%
16% H-1B Wage Gap vs. Comparable U.S. Workers Up to 35% at outsourcing firms · NBER Working Paper w34793, Feb 2026

The H-1B program requires employers to attest — through a Labor Condition Application — that they have tried to hire Americans, will pay the "prevailing wage," and will not adversely affect U.S. workers. The DOL certifies this application with minimal independent review. Wage levels can be set at Level I or II even for experienced roles, structurally anchoring pay well below actual market rates. There is no mandatory pre-filing recruitment for most H-1B petitions.

Once hired, workers face a structural trap: job loss triggers a 60-day grace period to find a new sponsor or leave the country. For Indian nationals, the EB-2 green card backlog currently sits at a 2013 priority date — a 12-year wait. This tether gives employers nearly total leverage over any worker who raises a complaint.

Section 03b — Right Branch

The H-2 Visa

Agricultural & seasonal workers: a different program, the same extraction. Employers simultaneously control the worker's visa status, housing, and income. A 60-day deportation clock enforces silence. Enforcement has collapsed while the program nearly tripled.

398,258 H-2A Positions Certified FY2025 Nearly 3× mid-2010s levels · DOL OFLC FY2025 Q4
200k–230k H-2B Certified Per Year (Typical) FY2026 Q1: ~39k certified · Non-agricultural seasonal
~99% DOL Certification Approval Rate Self-attested "labor market test" · GAO-25-106389
~90–92% H-2A Workers from Mexico Guatemala / Honduras / El Salvador ~5–8% combined · DOL OFLC FY2026 Q1
$1.7–2.1B Transferred from H-2A Workers to Employers (2026 IFR, One Year) EPI analysis Nov 2025
54% H-2A Share of All Agricultural Back-Wage Assessments FY2018–2023 · GAO-25-106389

The H-2A program requires employers to certify they tried to hire Americans, will pay the Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR), and will provide housing. The DOL certifies the application with minimal review — the same rubber-stamp process as H-1B, applied to physically demanding agricultural work.

A 2025 Interim Final Rule allows employers to deduct up to 30% of the AEWR for housing the workers are legally required to accept. Combined with meal deductions of up to $16.78/day and upfront recruiter fees of $1,000–$9,000+ (at loan rates up to 20%/month), workers arrive in debt and remain dependent. The employer controls both the housing and the immigration status simultaneously. Complaint, quit, or termination: the worker loses both their home and their right to remain in the United States in the same moment.

  • Recruiter Debt $1,000–$9,000+ upfront fees · loans at up to 20%/month interest · documented debt bondage · ProPublica 2025–2026
  • WHD Investigations Only 649 agricultural investigations in latest data · down >50% from a decade ago while program nearly tripled
  • Lucky Growers (CA, Dec 2025) Charged rent for pest-infested, structurally damaged housing · DOL recovered $171,400 back wages + $76,274 penalties
  • Operation Blooming Onion (GA, 2025–2026) $200M+ contractor profit Forced labor, violence, sexual assault, deaths — federal prosecutors called it "modern-day slavery" · DOJ / ProPublica
  • Farmworker Protection Rule Primary anti-retaliation enforcement tool suspended June 2025, proposed rescission July 2025 — removed during largest program expansion in history

Section 05b — Dichotomy of Truth

H-2: What Is Actually Happening

Agricultural and seasonal workers face the same extraction mechanism as H-1B workers — but with added physical danger, rural isolation, and employer-controlled housing. The enforcement apparatus has collapsed while the program tripled in size.

Who Are the H-2 Workers?

84% of H-2A jobs require lifting, exposure to extreme temperatures, and repetitive physical movements.

GAO-25-106389 — gao.gov/products/gao-25-106389

Employer controls housing AND visa status simultaneously. Complain, quit, or be terminated: lose your home and your right to remain in the United States in the same moment.

DOL Fact Sheet #26G · GAO-25-106389

Recruiter debt: $1,000–$9,000+ upfront. Loans at interest rates up to 20% per month. Documented debt bondage forces workers to endure abuse to repay family loans or avoid losing land back home — a classic trafficking indicator.

ProPublica 2025–2026 · Polaris Project Labor Trafficking Report

Absolute 3-year cap + 60-day mandatory departure. No pathway to permanence — unlike the H-1B green card track. Workers repeat this cycle across multiple seasons, accumulating debt with no path to stability.

Rural isolation cuts workers off from legal aid, labor advocates, and support networks. Polaris Project: immigration threats (deportation) used as the primary control tactic in 59% of foreign-national labor trafficking cases involving these visas.
