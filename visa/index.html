H-1

The H-1B program was written to fill genuine specialty occupation shortages. What it became is a self-attested, minimally audited labor pipeline approved 99% of the time. One employer controls the worker's income, visa status, and green card sponsorship simultaneously. The law describes a safeguard. The incentive structure built around it describes something else entirely.

Explore the H-1 chain → H-2

The H-2 program was written to provide temporary agricultural and seasonal workers when domestic labor is unavailable. What it became is a captive workforce. One employer controls housing, employment, and immigration status at the same moment. Recruiter debt begins before workers arrive. The 60-day deportation clock starts the moment they complain.

Media

The same raw government data gets interpreted in opposite directions depending on who funded the analysis. Think tanks on both sides cite the same USCIS approvals, DOL wage tables, and BLS unemployment figures — to opposite conclusions. Follow the funding and the disagreement resolves. The argument is not organic. It is manufactured. That is the point.
